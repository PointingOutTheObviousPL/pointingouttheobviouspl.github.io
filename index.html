Pointing Out the Obvious

Mental health accessibility for teens

About us

Mental health is an obvious problem in teens, yet few of them know where to get help or just don't feel comfortable asking for help. This is why we created POTO: to make quality mental health services more accessible! We also created an app that allows teens to talk to each other about sensitive topics anonymously without feeling nervous or embarrassed, as is so common with teens seeking mental help. Our team came about from the Aspen Challenge program, which challenges high school students to create and implement a solution to one of 5 topics. This year, 2024, PLHS chose to try and make mental health resources widely accessible to all students at no cost. Our hope is that POTO and SoulSync become reliable mental health hubs for teens to connect.

About our App

Our App, SoulSync, has chat rooms where teens seeking help can chat anonymously with therapists in training and other teens facing similar problems. Getting access to licensed therapists can be difficult, costly, and some people feel burdensome when asking for help. Our app solves all of these issues because it's super easy to access, it's free, and by participating you are actively training student therapists so they can later work professionally. We are hoping for this app to become accessible on school computers in SDUSD, but for now we are beta-testing on IOS and Android.

Resources

NAMI is designed for talking about sensitive topics with empathy, understanding and support. It is also a good referral line to connect you with more local resources.

The MHA organization is a giant hub of resources for mental health, including but not limited to, their immediate response resources, connections page (chat rooms, phone lines, etc.), and depression information page.

SAMHSA, Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration, is an official US organization with lots of resources for all sorts of scenarios.

This is just a SDUSD website featuring calming activities like yoga, live zoo cams, coloring, etc. It has lots of online and IRL things to try out that can help relieve stress and maybe even lead to a new hobby.

The Trevor project is a well known organization dedicated to helping LGBTQ+ youth. Call: 1-866-488-7386, Text: TREVOR to 1-202-304-1200

The Childhelp National Child Abuse Hotline has counselors who provide support, coping resources, and crisis counseling to abused children and anyone wanting more knowledge on what to do. Call/text: 800-422-4453

The NDVH is a hotline which helps people in abusive relationships, families, etc. There is a live chat directly on their website as well as phone numbers and resources.

This is an easy way to find therapists close to you.

This website is still under development, so new resources may be added periodically.
